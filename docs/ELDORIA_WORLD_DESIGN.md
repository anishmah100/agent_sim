# Eldoria — Procedural World Design

The default world the engine boots into. 1500×1500 tiles (2.25 M cells),
21 settlements, ~250 NPCs, ~14k decorations. Generated by
`engine/cmd/genworld_pretty/`; check in the JSON output to keep boots
deterministic.

## Macro layout

North → south band structure with low-frequency, large-amplitude
noise on the band boundaries so transitions feel organic rather than
banded. The shoreline on the east coast is its own noise envelope.

```
y =    0 ┬──────────────────────────────────┬
         │ Northern mountains (stone)       │   y < 240
         │   – Frostvale kingdom            │
         │   – Stonemoor, Coldbrook,        │
         │     Ironvein hamlets             │
         ├──────────────────────────────────┤
         │ Foothill grass + outcrops        │   y < 360
         │   – Mountainfoot                 │
         ├──────────────────────────────────┤
         │ Pinewood Forest (grass + pines)  │   y < 580
         │   – Pinewood kingdom             │
         │   – Aspendell, Mossglen,         │
         │     Birchwood, Riverbend         │
         ├──────────────────────────────────┤
         │ Central grasslands (grass +      │   y < 980
         │   patchy dirt fields)            │
         │   – CROSSROADS capital           │
         │   – Greenfield, Westgate,        │
         │     Oakshade, Eastfall, Greenrun │
         │   – Hawkspire, Saltwatch         │
         ├──────────────────────────────────┤
         │ Wetlands (grass + ponds)         │   y < 1200
         │   – Lakeshore kingdom            │
         │   – Highmarsh, Reedwater         │
         │   – Sunmarsh, Marshton           │
         ├──────────────────────────────────┤
         │ Sundered Dunes (sand + stone)    │   y ≥ 1200
         │   – Dunehallow kingdom           │
         │   – Sandholme, Driftwell         │
y = 1500 ┴──────────────────────────────────┘
                                       x → 1500 (east ocean past x≈1240)
```

Special features:

- **Lake Mirin** — irregular circle ~r 165 centred at (1000, 1000).
  Lakeshore sits on its SW shore; Saltport ferries goods across.
- **River Threads** — flows from the northern mountains south through
  Pinewood, then east into Lake Mirin, then out through the
  Sunmarsh wetlands into the east ocean.
- **East Ocean** — eastern coast past x ≈ 1240 with a noise-jittered
  shoreline; Saltport is the only city directly on it.
- **Road mesh** — every non-capital settlement has a road to
  Crossroads (radial), and pairs of geographically close settlements
  also have a road between them (so trade routes can choose).

## Settlement hierarchy

Three tiers control plaza size, building roster, and NPC archetype pool:

| Tier | Name | Plaza | Buildings | NPC count | Pool |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2 | **Royal Capital** (Crossroads) | r ≈ 44 | town hall, 6 cottages, smithy, granary, 6 different market stalls, 2 watchtowers, well | 14 | `capitalPool` — mayor, iron guards, trainers, wizard, baker, blacksmith, mason, child |
| 1 | **Regional Kingdom** (Frostvale, Pinewood, Saltport, Dunehallow, Lakeshore) | r ≈ 28 | 3 cottages, smithy, granary, 4 stalls, well, watchtower | 9 | `cityPool` — baker, blacksmith, mason, iron guard, trainers, cloaked wanderer, child, mayor |
| 0 | **Satellite village / hamlet** (15 of them) | r ≈ 18 | One of 5 layout templates picked by hash of `town.ref`: triangle, row, square, crossroads, tower hamlet | 4 | `villagePool` — baker, woodcutter, child, drifter, mason |

The five tier-0 layouts mean no two satellite villages look identical
at a glance; hash-of-ref keeps the choice deterministic across regen.

## Wilderness density

Three separate scatter passes give the "lived-in" feel:

1. **Kingdom sprawl** — each Tier 1 / 2 settlement gets 12-30 extra
   cottages + garden props within a 35-90 tile ring of the centre.
2. **`scatterFarms`** — ~640 standalone structures across the map
   (1 per ~3500 tiles). Each draws from a 5-way roll:
   - hamlet cluster (3 cottages + well + hay/logs/flowers)
   - lone cottage + garden patch
   - watchtower + nearby campfire
   - working farmstead (granary + 6 hay bales + 1 cottage)
   - blacksmith outpost + logs
3. **Vegetation pass** — biome-specific density, sprites picked by
   biome band (pines in the forest, cacti+dead trees in the desert,
   willows+reeds+lily pads in the wetlands, boulders in the mountains).

Wilderness NPCs (~90 of them) are placed by biome band too:
woodcutters cluster in the forest, drifters travel between towns,
goblins lurk in wilderness pockets, wizards in the desert.

## Determinism

- Single PRNG seed at the top of `main()`; the generator is pure
  output-equals-output across runs.
- All "random" decisions go through this PRNG. Token-bucket-style
  selection (hash of `town.ref`) is used wherever we want stable
  per-entity variation without mixing in PRNG state.
- The world JSON is checked in, so engine boots don't depend on the
  generator being runnable on the host.

## Regeneration runbook

```
cd ~/projects/agent_sim/engine
go run ./cmd/genworld_pretty -w 1500 -h 1500 -out ../worlds/eldoria.json
```

Optional flags:

- `-seed N` — change PRNG seed (default 42)
- `-w / -h` — width / height in tiles (must stay ≤ ~2000 for now;
  beyond that the frontend's LRU chunk cache needs more headroom)
- `-out PATH` — write somewhere other than the default

After regen:

1. Restart the engine on the new JSON.
2. Run the audit screenshots (`frontend/tests/_screenshot_audit.mjs`)
   and scan for: settlements on water tiles, stretched sprites,
   click-into-lamp regressions.
3. Commit the JSON.
